Should I repair my old computer or buy a new one?

This depends on your computer’s age, performance, and repair cost. Generally, if your system is more than 4–5 years old and requires major components like a motherboard or processor replacement, investing in a new computer is more cost-effective. However, if the issue is minor (like replacing RAM, SSD, or reinstalling Windows), repairing may extend your computer’s life at a lower cost.

Why does my computer overheat or shut down unexpectedly?

Overheating can occur due to dust buildup, poor ventilation, faulty fans, or dried-out thermal paste. When internal components exceed safe temperature limits, the system may shut down automatically to prevent damage. Cleaning the cooling system, improving airflow, or replacing the thermal compound can help maintain optimal temperatures.

Why is my computer running slow

A slow computer can result from various factors such as insufficient RAM, fragmented hard drives, malware infections, too many background applications, or outdated software. Over time, temporary files and system clutter can also reduce performance. Regular maintenance — including disk cleanup, upgrading memory (RAM or SSD), and scanning for viruses — can restore speed and efficiency.

How long should a computer last before replacement?

With regular maintenance, most computers last 5–7 years. However, as software and hardware requirements evolve, older systems may struggle to run modern applications. Upgrading components like RAM, storage, or graphics can extend the system’s lifespan affordably before a full replacement is necessary.

How can I protect my computer from viruses and malware?

Install a reputable antivirus or internet security suite, keep your operating system and software updated, avoid downloading unknown files or clicking suspicious links, and regularly back up your important data. Using strong passwords and enabling firewalls also adds extra protection against cyber threats.

What are the benefits of upgrading my RAM or storage?

Upgrading RAM (memory) allows your system to handle more applications simultaneously and improves multitasking. Upgrading to an SSD provides significantly faster boot times, quicker file access, and smoother performance. Together, these upgrades can make even older systems feel new again.

How can I improve my computer’s lifespan?

Keep your system clean, ensure proper ventilation, avoid overloading the hardware, install software updates regularly, and use surge protectors to prevent electrical damage. Consistent maintenance and responsible use can extend your computer’s life significantly.

What are the signs of a failing hard drive?

Common signs include unusual noises (clicking or grinding), frequent system crashes, missing files, slow performance, or corrupted data. If you notice these symptoms, back up your important data immediately and have your hard drive checked by a professional technician.

What is a custom-built PC, and is it better than a pre-built one?

A custom-built PC is assembled with components chosen specifically to match your needs — whether for gaming, design, or office work. It often provides better performance, easier upgrades, and higher value for money compared to most pre-built systems. However, pre-built PCs are more convenient for general users who prefer ready-to-use setups.

What should I do if my computer won’t start?

First, check the basics — ensure power cables and peripherals are properly connected, and verify that your monitor and power supply are functioning. If there are no signs of power, it could be a hardware issue (like a faulty motherboard, RAM, or PSU). In such cases, professional diagnosis is recommended to avoid further damage.

How do I choose the right computer for my needs?

Consider your purpose and usage:

For general use: a mid-range laptop or desktop with at least 8GB RAM and SSD storage.

For gaming or graphic design: a system with a dedicated graphics card (GPU) and higher-end processor.

For business use: reliable systems with strong multitasking capabilities and data security features.

Consulting a technician ensures you get the best configuration for your budget and requirements.
