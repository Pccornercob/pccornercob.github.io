    Should I repair my old computer or buy a new one?

    This depends on your computer’s age, performance, and repair cost. Generally, if your system is more than 4–5 years old and requires major components like a motherboard or processor replacement, investing in a new computer is more cost-effective. However, if the issue is minor (like replacing RAM, SSD, or reinstalling Windows), repairing may extend your computer’s life at a lower cost.

    Why does my computer overheat or shut down unexpectedly?

    Overheating can occur due to dust buildup, poor ventilation, faulty fans, or dried-out thermal paste. When internal components exceed safe temperature limits, the system may shut down automatically to prevent damage. Cleaning the cooling system, improving airflow, or replacing the thermal compound can help maintain optimal temperatures.

    Why is my computer running slow?

    A slow computer can result from various factors such as insufficient RAM, fragmented hard drives, malware infections, too many background applications, or outdated software. Over time, temporary files and system clutter can also reduce performance. Regular maintenance — including disk cleanup, upgrading memory (RAM or SSD), and scanning for viruses — can restore speed and efficiency.

    How long should a computer last before replacement?

    With regular maintenance, most computers last 5–7 years. However, as software and hardware requirements evolve, older systems may struggle to run modern applications. Upgrading components like RAM, storage, or graphics can extend the system’s lifespan affordably before a full replacement is necessary.
